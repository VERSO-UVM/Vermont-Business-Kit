Business Regulations in Vermont

Compliance with local, state, and federal regulations is essential for any business. This page outlines the key rules and requirements you need to know to stay legal and operate responsibly in Vermont.

State Business Registration

  • Vermont Secretary of State
    Register your business entity (LLC, corporation, nonprofit, etc.).
  • Trade Name (DBA) Filing
    Register a fictitious business name if operating under a name different from your own.

Tax Requirements

  • Vermont Department of Taxes – Sales & use tax, meals & rooms tax, income tax, and more.
  • Business Tax Registration – Required if you’re selling taxable goods or services.
  • Federal EIN (IRS) – Required for hiring employees or forming most business entities.

Licensing & Permits

  • Vermont Office of Professional Regulation – For licensed professions (e.g., electricians, barbers).
  • Environmental Permits – Required for construction, manufacturing, waste disposal, and more.
  • Local Zoning & Land Use – Check with your town’s zoning administrator for local restrictions.

Hiring & Employment Laws

  • Vermont Department of Labor – Information on minimum wage, hiring, workers' compensation, and more.
  • Unemployment Insurance Registration – Required for most employers.
  • Workplace Safety (VOSHA) – Vermont Occupational Safety and Health Administration resources.

Special Industry Regulations

Certain industries are subject to additional oversight. If your business operates in any of the following areas, be sure to check for additional compliance requirements:

  • Food production & restaurants
  • Childcare and education
  • Agriculture and hemp/CBD production
  • Alcohol sales or distribution
  • Construction and trades

See full industry compliance guide

Development & Environmental Regulations (Act 250)

If your business involves development, large-scale construction, or environmental impact, you may need to apply for an Act 250 permit—a unique Vermont law designed to protect the state’s natural resources.
